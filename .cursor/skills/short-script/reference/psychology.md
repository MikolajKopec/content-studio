# Content Psychology Reference

## Curiosity Gap (Loewenstein, 1994)

Curiosity = discomfort from the gap between what you know and what you want to know.

**Goldilocks principle:**
- Too small: "3 tips for better stories" — boring, no tension
- Too large: "The secret of the universe" — too abstract
- Optimal: "One mistake in stories that cost me 80% reach — and how I fixed it in 5 minutes"

**Application patterns:**
- Show result, hide process: "I got [result]. Here's exactly what I did."
- Give 2 of 3: list two points, delay the third — "and the last one is what actually makes it work"
- Contrast: show before → build tension → after at the end

## Open Loops (Zeigarnik Effect, 1927)

Unfinished tasks occupy working memory ~2x more than completed ones. Open loops = "mental browser tabs."

**Three levels:**

1. **Master Loop** (entire piece) — open in hook, close at the end
2. **Segment Loops** (mid-content) — "but before I show you the result..." → deliver other value → close → open next
3. **Cross-Content Loops** (between pieces) — "In part 2, I'll show the advanced version..."

**Loop phrases:**
- "...and here's why that matters..."
- "...but that's not even the crazy part..."
- "...wait, it gets better/worse..."
- "...now watch what happens when..."

**Rules:**
- Never have more than 3 open simultaneously (cognitive overload)
- Always close with payoff (bad payoff = lost trust)
- Closing one loop should open the next

## STEPPS Framework (Jonah Berger, "Contagious", 2013)

People share content for what it says about THEM, not about the content itself.

| Factor | Meaning | How to apply |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **S**ocial Currency | Makes sharer look smart/insider | Give info they can "pull out" in conversation |
| **T**riggers | Connected to daily situations | Link to morning routines, deadlines, scrolling |
| **E**motion | High-arousal emotions drive sharing | Awe, anxiety, anger, excitement — NOT sadness or calm |
| **P**ublic | Visible behavior gets copied | Create content people want on their profile |
| **P**ractical Value | Useful = shareable | Checklists, frameworks, systems > loose tips |
| **S**tories | Narrative = 22x more memorable | Your content = Trojan Horse (story is the horse, lesson is the soldier) |

**Audit:** Minimum 3 of 6 = high viral potential.

## SUCCESs Framework (Heath & Heath, "Made to Stick")

| Element | Rule | Application |
|---------|------|-------------|
| **S**imple | Find the core | One piece = one idea |
| **U**nexpected | Break patterns | Pattern interrupt in hook + counterintuitive insight |
| **C**oncrete | Specificity > abstraction | "347 leads in 14 days" not "many more clients" |
| **C**redible | Prove it | Numbers, screenshots, anti-authority |
| **E**motional | Feel > think | "Imagine that..." — transport the viewer |
| **S**tories | Wrap in narrative | Every framework in a transformation story |

## Cognitive Biases for Content

**Loss Aversion** (Kahneman & Tversky): Pain of loss is ~2x stronger than pleasure of gain.
- Frame through loss: "Stop losing 5h/week on content that doesn't work" > "Learn this framework"

**Anchoring** (Tversky & Kahneman): First info = anchor for all evaluation.
- Start big, show small: "Agencies charge $5000 for this. This framework gives you 80% of the result in 15 minutes for free."

**Social Proof** (Cialdini): In uncertainty, people copy others.
- Cite numbers, show transformations, screenshot DMs/comments.

**Bizarreness Effect** (McDaniel & Einstein): Unusual info is better remembered.
- Unexpected metaphors, unusual comparisons, cross-domain analogies.

## 7 Hook Archetypes

| Archetype | Mechanism | Template |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Contrarian** | Pattern interrupt + curiosity gap | "Stop [POPULAR ADVICE]. That's why [BAD RESULT]." |
| **Proof-first** | Social proof + anchoring | "[RESULT] in [SHORT TIME]. Here's exactly how." |
| **Enemy** | In-group/out-group bias | "Nobody in [INDUSTRY] will tell you this because..." |
| **Question** | Instinct to answer | "Why doesn't your [X] work even though you do everything right?" |
| **Story-entry** | Transportation theory | "Yesterday at 3am I got a message that changed everything." |
| **Direct command** | Authority + interrupt | "Stop. What I'm about to tell you will change how you do [X]." |
| **Bizarre** | Bizarreness effect + curiosity | "[ABSURD STATEMENT]. And I'm serious." |

## Pattern Interrupts

The brain filters repetitive stimuli (habituation). Unexpected stimulus forces attention shift, activates amygdala, increases dopamine.

| Type | What | When | Frequency |
|------|------|------|-----------|
| Jump Cut | Mid-sentence cut to different angle | Every 3-5s | Most common |
| Sound Effect | Short whoosh/pop/ding on transition | At point changes | Every 7-10s |
| Text Pop-up | Animated keyword on screen | At key concepts | Every 5-8s |
| B-roll Flash | 1-2s illustrative insert | At each new point | Every 8-12s |
| Zoom Punch | Sudden zoom on face/object | On punchline | 2-3x per video |
| Speed Ramp | Speed up → slow down | On process/montage | Situational |

**3-second rule:** No shot should last longer than 3 seconds without some visual change element.
