# Platform-Specific Reference

## TikTok
- Hook in 1s (not 3s) — most impatient audience
- Native look > polished look (iPhone > camera)
- Trending sounds increase distribution, custom audio builds brand
- Duet/Stitch = fastest growth hack (piggyback on others' audience)
- Reply-to-comment videos = highest engagement rate format
- Optimal lengths: 15s, 30s, 60s (under 60s generally performs best)

## Instagram Reels
- Polished > raw (opposite of TikTok)
- Carousel + Reel combo = best reach in 2025-2026
- Reels with on-screen text perform better (many watch without sound)
- Share-to-DM metric is key — create content people want to send to friends
- Caption matters more than on TikTok — use it for SEO and context
- Optimal lengths: 15s, 30s, 60s, 90s max

## YouTube Shorts
- Algorithm favors watch time % AND rewatch rate
- Shorts that end "too early" (viewer wants to rewatch) get best algorithmic push
- Linking Shorts to long-form = best monetization strategy
- YouTube rewards Shorts with higher subscriber conversion rate
- Vertical format, max 60s
- Title still matters (unlike TikTok where caption is secondary)

## LinkedIn (posts)
- Hook must fit in "see more" preview — 2-3 lines on mobile (~210 characters)
- Max 1-2 sentences per paragraph; empty lines between paragraphs
- No links in post body (algorithm penalizes) — link in first comment
- Emoji sparingly — max 1-2 in entire post, or zero
- Casual-professional tone wins; pure corporate gets ignored

## Twitter/X
- 280 chars per tweet; no hashtags (algorithm doesn't use them for discovery)
- 1 tweet = 1 complete idea; don't continue across tweets
- Threads optimal at 7-15 tweets; test as single tweets first
- Quote tweets and replies = best engagement strategy

## Instagram (Feed, Carousel)
- Slide 1 must work as standalone image in feed
- Max 30-40 words per slide; large font (readable without zooming)
- Hashtags: 3-5 relevant ones, end of caption or first comment
- "Save" + "share to DM" are the metrics that matter

## CTA Strategies by Effectiveness (short-form video)

| Level | CTA | Conversion | When to use |
|-------|-----|-----------|-------------|
| 1 (weak) | "Follow for more" | ~0.5-1% | Never as sole CTA |
| 2 | "Save this for later" | ~2-4% | Lists, tutorials, tips |
| 3 | "Comment [word] and I'll send you [X]" | ~3-6% | Lead gen, engagement |
| 4 | "Link in bio for the full [guide/template]" | ~1-3% | Traffic driving |
| 5 (strong) | "Part 2 drops [day] — follow so you don't miss it" | ~5-8% | Series content |
| 6 (strongest) | Implicit — video ends with open loop, bio has link | Variable | Advanced |

**Rule:** CTA should be a LOGICAL next step, not a request.

## Embedded CTA Techniques
1. **Mid-roll (15-20s mark):** "By the way, if this is useful, I have a free [X] — link in bio — anyway, back to point 3..."
2. **Stack CTA:** CTA is part of content: "Step 4 is in the free guide in my bio because it needs more detail"
3. **Repeat CTA:** On-screen text (pinned "FREE GUIDE → BIO") + verbal CTA at end

## CTA by Goal (social posts)

| Goal | CTA Type | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| Comments | Opinion-forcing question | "Agree or disagree?" / "Which one resonates?" |
| Shares | Identity + value | "Share with someone who needs this" |
| Saves | Practical value | "Save this — you'll need it" |
| Traffic | Value bridge | "Full breakdown in the link (first comment)" |
| Follows | Series tease | "Follow for Part 2 on [day]" |

## Controversy Spectrum (for driving comments)

| Level | Type | Example | Risk |
|-------|------|---------|------|
| 1 Safe | Preference | "Planning vs spontaneous content — which do you prefer?" | Zero |
| 2 Mild | Light opinion | "I think daily publishing is a trap" | Minimal |
| 3 Moderate | Consensus violation | "Storytelling is overrated. Here's why." | Medium |
| 4 Sharp | Attacking sacred cows | "The entire personal branding industry is lying." | High |
| 5 Toxic | Personal attacks | (never use) | Destructive |

**Optimum: level 2-3** — generates discussion without destruction.

## Comment-Driving Techniques
- **Identity-based:** "There are two types of creators: [A] and [B]. Which one are you?"
- **Completion drive:** "Finish this sentence: The biggest problem with content is ___"
- **Rating:** "Rate on a scale of 1-10"
- **Opinion-forcing:** "Unpopular opinion: [statement]. Agree?"
- **Vulnerable ask:** "Honestly? I struggle with this. How do you solve it?"
